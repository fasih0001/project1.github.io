Fruit fly detection and classification in IoT setup

Abstract

This project aims to address the challenges faced by farmers and agricultural experts in identifying pests in orchards. Traditionally, visual inspection was used, which was a subjective and time-consuming process. Recent advancements in deep learning-based computer vision have shown great potential in solving problems related to image recognition and detection in agriculture. The product developed in this project targets specific fruit fly species and uses an IoT solution to provide warnings or alerts to users anywhere in the world through a mobile application.
The FlyTrap application is designed to facilitate the monitoring and management of fruit flies by providing real-time data on their activities and infestation levels. It is equipped with several features, including a counting feature, real-time alerts, and reporting features that generate detailed reports on infestation levels. The application provides farmers and agriculturalists with valuable information that can guide their management strategies and prevent future crop damage and losses. The advanced machine learning algorithms and intuitive interface make FlyTrap a valuable tool for optimizing crop yields and reducing losses caused by fruit fly infestations.

Deep learning approach

Introducing an efficient approach to address the problem of low inference speed on IoT devices (e.g NVIDIA Jetson Nano) running YOLOv5 while maintain- ing sufficient accuracy. We propose modifications to an existing YOLOv5 architecture to achieve superior results while reducing the floating point operations (FLOPs) significantly.
I modified the existing YOLOv5s architecture to achieve 3.5GFLOPs while achieving higher map@0.5-0.95, which is the overall mean average precision at IoU values ranging from 50% to 95%. The hyperparameters ramaine d constant for comparisions. I modified the YOLOv5s architectrue at the backbone and prediction level to achieve significant drop in compuational complexity of the algorithm. This was primarily done for optimal deployment on Nvidia Jetson Nano.
